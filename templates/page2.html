Spooky Southampton

The Medieval Merchant's House, 58 French Street

One of the earliest surviving houses in England and resident to many mischevous spirits, bowing out candles in the cellar, watching you while you sleep, strange footsteps, ghostly shapes, doors slamming and being pused by unseen hands have all been reported at within this mish mash of occupancies.

The Tudor House Museum

The sight of several paranormal investigations

Reports of several figures seen walking across rooms and through briked up doorways, lounging within the walls, and playing with the lights!

The Station Pub, Bitterne

Beware a tap on the shoulders in the ladies facilities, thought to be the regular who never left, and the solitary man seen by the snooker table guarding by night.

Flying plates, whistling in the gents, and a store room whos contents is thrown around upon the door closing!

The Red Lion Pub, High Street, Southampton

The scene of the treason trial of the 'Southampton Plot' conspirators, who plotted the death of Henry V in 1415. All found guilty, and beheaded. Numerous sightings have been reported of a ghostly procession of mournful people leaving the pub.

Beware also of the the lady drifting through the bar and heavy footsteps in the cellar!
